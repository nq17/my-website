What is Depression?

Depression is a common mental health disorder that causes persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest. It affects how a person thinks, feels, and handles daily activities.

Common Symptoms of Depression

  • Feeling sad or empty most of the day
  • Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Trouble sleeping or sleeping too much
  • Feeling tired or having low energy
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is a feeling of fear, worry, or unease. A little anxiety is normal, but when it becomes constant and overwhelming it can interfere with daily life. Anxiety disorders are the most common mental health conditions in the United States.

Common Symptoms of Anxiety

  • Feeling nervous, restless, or tense
  • Sense of danger or panic
  • Increased heart rate
  • Rapid breathing or shortness of breath
  • Sweating or trembling
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Avoiding things that trigger anxiety

How are They Related?

Depression and anxiety often occur together. Many people who have depression also experience anxiety, and vice versa. Both conditions share some symptoms like trouble sleeping and difficulty concentrating. It is important to talk to a doctor or mental health professional if you think you have either condition.

How to Get Help

If you or someone you know is struggling with depression or anxiety, there are options available:

  • Talk to your doctor - They can refer you to a mental health professional or discuss treatment options.
  • Therapy - Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is one of the most effective treatments for both depression and anxiety.
  • Medication - A doctor may prescribe antidepressants or anti-anxiety medication.
  • Support groups - Talking to others who understand what you are going through can help.
  • Lifestyle changes - Regular exercise, a healthy diet, and getting enough sleep can make a big difference.

Crisis Resources

If you are in crisis, please reach out right away:

  • Call or text 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline)
  • Text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line)
  • Call 911 if you are in immediate danger
